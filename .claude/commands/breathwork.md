## Respiratory Mechanics Model

### 360° Breathing vs. Chest-Dominant Breathing
```
Chest-dominant (this athlete's pattern):
- Ribs elevate on inhale → rib flare reinforced
- Diaphragm compressed → reduced descent
- TVA inhibited (antagonist relationship with compressed diaphragm)
- IAP (intra-abdominal pressure) reduced → spinal stability compromised

360° breathing (target pattern):
- Ribs stay down on inhale
- Diaphragm descends fully → IAP increases
- TVA co-activates with diaphragm
- Obliques and back muscles expand (360° circumferential)
- Pelvis and spine stabilized from inside
```

### The IAP System (why breathing is the foundation)
Intra-abdominal pressure = the body's internal spine stabilizer. Generated by:
- Diaphragm descending (creates the "piston" pressure)
- TVA gently drawing in (creates the "cylinder" around the spine)
- Pelvic floor co-contracting (closes the bottom of the cylinder)

When breathing is chest-dominant, this system doesn't pressurize. Every exercise — deadbug, skating push, golf swing — happens without proper spinal support.

### Nervous System Regulation (equally important for this athlete)
The breath is the fastest lever on the autonomic nervous system available without drugs.

```
Stressed / fatigued state (this athlete's evening baseline):
- Sympathetic dominance
- Short, chest-dominant breaths
- Elevated cortisol
- Motivation to train: low

Target state for training entry:
- Parasympathetic shift
- Slow, 360° nasal breathing
- 5–7 breaths/minute (Coherence breathing range)
- Nervous system calm enough to engage witness mode
```

The pre-session breath protocol is not just warm-up — it is a state-change tool.

## Core Protocols

### Protocol 1 — TVA Activation Breath (already in program)
*Use: Phase 1 warm-up, before every exercise set*
```
Inhale: 4 counts, nasal, expand into back and obliques (NOT ribs up)
Hold: 1 count at full expansion — feel the 360° pressure
Exhale: 4–6 counts, slow, maintain rib position, light TVA draw
Pause: 2 counts — feel the residual engagement
```
Cue: "Breathe into your back pockets." The ribs don't move. The belly expands 360°.

### Protocol 2 — Evening Re-Entry Breath (Tonight Mode gateway)
*Use: Before any session when fatigued or stressed*
```
3 breaths, no timer pressure:
Inhale: let it come in slowly through the nose
Exhale: longer than the inhale — sigh it out if needed
After 3 breaths: check in — where do you feel your body right now?
```
This is the re-entry door. Not a performance protocol. Just contact with the body.
If this produces a feeling of "arriving" even slightly — that's Tonight Mode access.

### Protocol 3 — Coherence Breathing (between sets or pre-golf)
*Use: Between exercise sets, pre-shot routine, pre-ice session*
```
Inhale: 5 counts (nasal, 360°)
Exhale: 5 counts (nasal or pursed lips)
Repeat 5–10 cycles
```
This pattern (5s/5s) produces heart rate variability resonance — the measurable marker of nervous system coherence. One minute of this between sets changes your physiological state more than rest alone.

### Protocol 4 — Performance Breath (pre-exertion)
*Use: Before explosive work — skater bounds, lateral jumps*
```
Inhale: 4 counts, full 360° expansion
Hold: 2 counts — pressurize the IAP system
Exhale: Begin the movement at the start of the exhale (not after)
```
The movement starts on the exhale — not before it, not after it. This times the IAP peak with the demand.

## Breathing + Posture Connection
Always explain:
- **Rib flare** = a breathing dysfunction made visible. Chest-dominant breathing trains the ribs to elevate. Fixing the breathing pattern gradually reduces the rib flare.
- **TVA activation** can only happen reliably when the diaphragm has full range of motion. You cannot cue your way to TVA activation with compressed breathing.
- **Anterior pelvic tilt** is partly maintained by the breathing dysfunction — a compressed diaphragm keeps pulling the thorax forward.
