## Golf Biomechanics Model (Left-Handed)

### The Swing Chain (left-handed framing)
```
Setup/posture → Backswing hip turn (right hip loads) → Top of swing → 
Downswing: left hip clears → Weight transfers left foot → 
Through swing: left glute fires, right side releases → Finish
```

For this athlete:
- **Backswing**: Anterior pelvic tilt + right knee valgus = limited right hip load. The hip lifts instead of turning.
- **Downswing**: Weak TVA = spine sways rather than rotates. Power leaks.
- **Through swing**: Serratus/rib control loss = left shoulder rises early, path goes right (increases fade).

### The Fade (leave it alone, build around it)
Natural left-hand fade = slightly open face at impact, path slightly in-to-out.
It is mechanically efficient for this player. The goal is consistency, distance, and control — not eliminating shape.
Distance upside when posture improves: mini driver could move to 240–250.

### The Pre-Shot Breath Protocol
One controlled breath before each shot serves two functions:
1. Activates TVA and diaphragm — creates core pressure before movement
2. Down-regulates nervous system — reduces "mental scatter" during tired evening rounds

Pattern: 4-count nasal inhale (expand into back/obliques) → 4-count slow exhale → swing. Same breath as Phase 1 TVA exercise.

### Three Core Range Drills
**Split Stance Swing Drill**: Narrow stance, one foot back on toe. Forces proper hip rotation because there's no way to sway. 10 swings before full-width swings.

**Step-Through Drill**: Start with feet together, step into impact as you swing. Builds hip clearance timing. Mirrors the lateral chain push exercise directly.

**Pre-Round Glute Activation**: 10 banded lateral walks + 10 lateral step-downs before hitting the first ball. Wakes up glute medius so hip clearance is available from hole 1.

## Drill Design Principles
When designing golf drills for this athlete:
1. **Isolate the pelvis** — most swing faults trace back to pelvic/TVA dysfunction for this player
2. **Left-handed framing** — always describe as left-handed: left hip clears, right leg is trail
3. **Awareness cue** — what does proper feel like? (e.g., "feel the ground through the left heel")
4. **Connect to body** — every drill connects to at least one rehab exercise they already know
