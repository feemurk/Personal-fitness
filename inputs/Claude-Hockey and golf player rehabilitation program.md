---

Phase 1 — Breathing and TVA Activation (10–12 minutes)

This phase restores proper rib cage–pelvis alignment and deep core engagement so the legs can generate force efficiently without the spine compensating.

Supine TVA Breathing

Sets: 3
Breaths per set: 5–6 slow breaths

How to perform:

Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat. Keep the lower back gently contacting the floor. Inhale slowly through your nose and allow the breath to expand into your back and obliques instead of lifting the ribs upward. Exhale slowly while lightly engaging the TVA and maintaining contact between the lower back and the floor.

Focus: The goal is to create 360-degree abdominal expansion while keeping the ribs down, which teaches the diaphragm, obliques, and TVA to work together to stabilize the pelvis.

Expected impact: This exercise improves core pressure and spinal stability, which helps maintain balance and force transfer during skating strides.


---

Deadbug with Leg Extension

Sets: 3
Reps: 6 per side

How to perform:

Remain on your back with TVA engagement established from the breathing exercise. Slowly extend one leg while maintaining rib control and pelvic stability, then return and repeat on the other side.

Focus: The purpose is to teach the core to remain stable while the legs move, mimicking how your body must behave during skating pushes.

Expected impact: Improved pelvic control and reduced energy leakage during skating strides.


---

Phase 2 — Serratus and Scapular Stability (10 minutes)

This phase connects your rib cage stability to shoulder positioning, preventing upper-body tension and allowing the torso to remain stable while the legs generate force.

Wall Y Raises

Sets: 3
Reps: 10

How to perform:

Stand against a wall with arms raised in a Y shape and slide them upward while keeping your ribs down and shoulders relaxed.

Focus: Emphasize lower trap and serratus activation while preventing the shoulders from shrugging upward.

Expected impact: Improves scapular control and posture, which keeps the upper body quiet and balanced while skating.


---

Plank with Serratus Push

Sets: 3
Duration: 30 seconds

Every 5–6 seconds perform a small scapular push-up by pushing the floor away and spreading the shoulder blades.

Focus: Maintain a long spine and engaged core while activating the serratus to control the shoulder blades.

Expected impact: Builds torso stiffness and shoulder stability, which allows better transfer of power from the hips to the ice.


---

Serratus Wall Press Hold

Sets: 3
Duration: 20 seconds

Press both hands into a wall with straight arms and actively push your shoulder blades forward.

Focus: Think about wrapping the shoulder blades around the rib cage while keeping the neck relaxed and ribs down.

Expected impact: Improves serratus strength and rib cage stability, which supports the breathing and TVA work you are already doing.


---

Phase 3 — Lower Body Strength and Knee Alignment (15–20 minutes)

This phase rebuilds quad strength, hip stability, and knee tracking, which are critical for skating power and recovering from patellar tendon issues.

Bulgarian Split Squats

Sets: 3
Reps: 6–8

How to perform:

Rear foot elevated on a bench. Lower slowly and push through the front leg to stand.

Focus: Maintain controlled descent while allowing the knee to travel forward over the toes without collapsing inward.

Expected impact: Builds single-leg strength and skating push power, particularly important for restoring symmetry between legs.


---

Spanish Squats

Sets: 3
Reps: 10

How to perform:

Use a strap or band behind the knees and sit back into a squat while keeping the torso upright.

Focus: Keep tension in the strap and allow the quads to load while maintaining upright posture.

Expected impact: Strengthens the quadriceps safely while reducing stress on the patellar tendon.


---

Lateral Step Downs

Sets: 3
Reps: 8 per leg

How to perform:

Stand on a step and slowly lower the opposite foot toward the floor.

Focus: Keep the knee tracking outward over the middle toes rather than collapsing inward.

Expected impact: Directly trains control against knee valgus, which you noted occurs in both knees.


---

Phase 4 — Hip Control and Skating Power (10 minutes)

This phase integrates strength into lateral force production similar to skating pushes.

Golf Driver Lateral Chain Push

Sets: 3
Reps: 10 pushes per side

How to perform:

Take a wide stance like preparing to swing a golf driver and push outward through the foot while driving the knee outward.

Focus: Feel the foot arch, ankle, knee, and glute working together to push the ground sideways.

Expected impact: Reinforces the entire lateral kinetic chain used in skating pushes.


---

Banded Monster Walks

Sets: 3
Steps: 10 each direction

How to perform:

With a resistance band around ankles or knees, step sideways while maintaining tension.

Focus: Keep knees pushed outward and hips engaged while taking controlled steps.

Expected impact: Strengthens the glute medius and hip stabilizers, which prevent inward knee collapse.


---

Lateral Skater Bounds

Sets: 3
Reps: 8 per side

How to perform:

Jump sideways from one leg to the other and stabilize on landing.

Focus: Land softly while keeping the knee aligned over the foot and controlling the landing.

Expected impact: Develops explosive skating power and lateral force generation.


---

Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift

Sets: 3
Reps: 8 per leg

How to perform:

Stand on one leg and hinge forward at the hips while extending the other leg behind.

Focus: Maintain hip alignment and balance while activating the glutes and hamstrings.

Expected impact: Improves posterior chain strength and balance during glide phases.
